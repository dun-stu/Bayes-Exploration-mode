# Rubric Reference — Sections Relevant During Development

Extracted from `project_assessment.md`. These are the assessment criteria most likely to be relevant when judging whether something that happens during development is worth recording for the report.

---

## Report: Technical Quality, Methodology and Evaluation (35% of Report mark)

This assesses the description of the main output from the project (for example, software, research, hardware, teaching concepts).

Where appropriate, the student has used standard accepted methods of describing designs (e.g. architecture diagrams, UML, database schemas, algorithms, mathematics, etc.). Alternatively, an attempt has been made to formalise the description.

This part should cover steps taken to evaluate, test or compare the work to the state of the art. Does the student demonstrate a level of understanding of the theory, design and technical aspects of the project? Key decisions should be highlighted and justified.

An evaluation of the work should be included. This includes correctness, usability, fitness for purpose, ranking with similar work, and so on. It may include manual and automated testing, user surveys, and some sort of performance metric, for example. Has the student demonstrated a suitable level of critical analysis and evaluation?

**Key assessment points:**
- Understanding of the material
- Technical quality
- Requirements analysis
- Use of design methodologies as appropriate
- Testing as appropriate
- Suitable evaluation of the work
- Critical analysis

**Band descriptors (abbreviated):**
- **60–69:** Technical aspects described well, good understanding demonstrated. Testing and evaluation well thought-through. Report could be used to reproduce the work.
- **70–79:** Very good description with formality applied. Testing and evaluation very well thought-through. Report could be used to reproduce the work.
- **80–89:** Designs very well considered, clear, easy to understand. Evaluated to a very high level. Very thorough understanding demonstrated and explained very clearly.
- **90–100:** Very little to fault. Extremely thorough understanding. Explanations exceptional.

## Report: Summary and Conclusions (15% of Report mark)

**Key assessment points:**
- Summary of the work
- Discussion of achievements
- Critical reflection
- Ideas for further work
- Alignment with abstract and introduction

**Relevant to development:** Critical reflection on what worked and what didn't. The ability to see weaknesses and give good solutions to problems. Ideas for future work that are ambitious, relevant, and well thought out.

---

## Achievements: Complexity (33% of Achievements mark)

This assesses the difficulty of the work.

**Key assessment points:**
- Difficulty level of work
- Use of existing ideas
- Originality

**Band descriptors (abbreviated):**
- **60–69:** Brings together ideas from several areas, or delves fairly deeply into one technical area.
- **70–79:** Solves a significant problem or combines ideas from several areas in an interesting way. Makes some use of material from the scientific literature.
- **80–89:** Additionally builds on material from the scientific literature.
- **90–100:** Additionally solves a long-standing hard problem or combines ideas from several areas in an original way.

---

## Achievements: Scale (33% of Achievements mark)

Assesses the amount of work that has gone into the project work itself.

**Key assessment points:**
- Amount of work
- Difficulties overcome
- Acquiring of knowledge
- Breadth of coverage (e.g. in experiments, development or testing)

**Band descriptors (abbreviated):**
- **60–69:** Took a good amount of time. Various difficulties analysed and overcome.
- **70–79:** Considerable effort. Difficult problems overcome and solutions demonstrated so they can be repeated.
- **80–89:** Some original thought. Ideas not just a repeat of existing work — good effort to analyse and build upon other works. Outputs clearly show the amount of work involved and are complete and polished.
- **90–100:** Ownership taken of the problems. More reading performed. Full understanding developed of where the work sits. Obvious that something much greater could be delivered given more time.

---

## Achievements: Achievement (34% of Achievements mark)

Assesses how well the aims were met. Combines how difficult the stated aims were and how much has been achieved trying to meet those aims.

**Key assessment points:**
- Achievements
- Difficulty of achievements

**Band descriptors (abbreviated):**
- **60–69:** All set aims either met or well-documented reason why not.
- **70–79:** Additionally, the given objectives were challenging.
- **80–89:** Additionally, the presented solution is convincing and based on suitable principles from the literature.
- **90–100:** Additionally, the provided solution is hard to improve upon.

## How to Use This During Development

When judging whether a development event is report-relevant, ask:

- **Technical Quality:** Does this involve a key decision that should be highlighted and justified? Does it demonstrate understanding of the technical aspects? Would describing this help a reader reproduce the work?
- **Scale:** Does this represent a difficulty that was overcome? Does it show breadth of coverage or effort?
- **Complexity:** Does this demonstrate use of ideas from the literature, or combining ideas from several areas?
- **Achievement:** Does this contribute to meeting the project aims convincingly?

If the answer to any of these is yes, flag it. If borderline, consult the user.
